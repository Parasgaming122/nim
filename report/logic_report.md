## 2. Demonstrated Logical Principles
Through Nim, we demonstrated three core principles:

*   **Deductive Reasoning (Mathematical Certainty):** At the heart of Nim lies the "Nim-sum"—the binary digital sum of heap sizes. Using our digital tool, we showed that any game state is either a "Winning (N)" or "Losing (P)" position. Given a specific state, one can logically deduce the outcome assuming perfect play. For instance, we demonstrated that a 3-5-7 setup has a Nim-sum of 1, meaning the first player has a guaranteed path to victory if they follow a deductive strategy.
*   **Systematic Elimination:** Critical thinking involves narrowing down possibilities to reach a goal. In Nim, players must analyze rods and decide which balls to shift. We showed that logical play involves systematically eliminating moves that lead to a non-zero Nim-sum, thereby forcing the opponent into a losing state.
*   **Anticipatory Thinking (Risk Assessment):** Strategic thinking requires analyzing consequences before acting. We illustrated this by showing how a player must anticipate an opponent's responses. By selecting a specific range of balls, a player recalculates the "risk" of leaving their opponent with a winning move, demonstrating real-time reasoning based on the game's current stability.

## 3. Challenges and Strategies
A major challenge was translating abstract binary XOR logic (the Nim-sum) into an accessible 15-minute presentation. Most classmates were unfamiliar with binary arithmetic, making the mathematical proof behind Nim difficult to convey verbally.

To overcome this, we developed a "Logic Mode" within our digital game. This toggleable HUD visualized the Nim-sum and provided strategic hints in plain English. This allowed us to engage the class visually while explaining the underlying principles, effectively bridging the gap between complex theory and hands-on interaction.
