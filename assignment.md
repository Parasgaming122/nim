ASSIGNMENT # 1 TITLE: LOGIC IN ACTION: DEMONSTRATING CRITICAL THINKING THROUGH GAMES   | Issued April 9, 2026
Total Marks: 10
Part 1: Individual  Activity 
Instructions to Students :
    1. Choose One Activity: Your group must select one of the following activities: Puzzle (e.g., Sudoku, logic grid puzzle), Jenga, Chess, Riddle Solving (a series of interconnected riddles), or Monopoly etc.
    2. Demonstrate Logical Principles: Plan how you will use your chosen activity to demonstrate specific principles of logic and critical thinking during a 15-20minute presentation within the 2-hour class. Consider the following: 
        ◦ Puzzle: Deduction, pattern recognition, systematic elimination, hypothesis testing.
        ◦ Jenga: Inductive reasoning (predicting stability), understanding cause and effect, risk assessment.
        ◦ Chess/Checkers: Strategic thinking, planning, analyzing consequences, identifying fallacies (e.g., hasty generalization about opponent's strategy).
        ◦ Monopoly: Strategic decision-making, understanding probability (dice rolls), analyzing risks and rewards, identifying potential logical fallacies in opponents' arguments.
        ◦ Hangman: The game of Hangman can be a surprisingly effective tool to illustrate and practice fundamental principles of logic and critical thinking.
        ◦ Sequence: The board game "Sequence" can be a valuable tool for illustrating and practicing various aspects of logic and critical thinking, particularly relevant to the problem-solving nature of Electrical Engineering.
        ◦ 
    3. Engage the Class: Your presentation should actively involve the rest of the class. This could include: 
        ◦ Explaining the logical principles before or during the activity.
        ◦ Asking the class questions related to the logic involved.
        ◦ Having classmates participate in solving a part of the puzzle or making a move in the game.
        ◦ Presenting the riddles for the class to solve.
    4. Time Management: Your demonstration and explanation must fit within the allocated 15-20 minutes. Practice beforehand.
    5. Creativity: Be creative in how you present and engage the class.
Marking Rubric for Group Activity (6 Marks):
    • Demonstration of Logical Principles: Clarity and accuracy in explaining and demonstrating relevant logical and critical thinking concepts through the chosen activity.
    • Engagement of the Class: Effectiveness in involving the audience and making the activity interactive.
    • Planning and Organization: Evidence of clear planning, efficient use of time, and smooth execution of the activity. Arrangement of the game in physical form like chess board,  Jenga or Monopoly, Sequence etc. 
    • Understanding of the Activity: Demonstrated understanding of the chosen activity and its inherent logical elements.
    • Presentation: In-class presentations of the group activities (allocate approximately 20 minutes per group within the 2-hour class).
Part 2: Written Report (4 Marks)
Instructions to Students (Group Report):
Each student must individually submit a written report (400-550 words) reflecting on their group's activity and its connection to logic and critical thinking. The report should include the following sections:
    1. Introduction (Brief): State the chosen activity and the group members. Briefly outline the purpose of the report.
    2. Demonstrated Logical Principles: 
        ◦ Clearly identify and explain at least three specific principles of logic and critical thinking that your group aimed to demonstrate through the activity.
        ◦ Provide concrete examples from your group's presentation of how these principles were illustrated during the activity.
    3. Challenges and Strategies: 
        ◦ Discuss any challenges your group faced in planning or executing the demonstration.
        ◦ Explain the strategies your group employed to overcome these challenges and effectively convey the logical concepts.
    4. Reflection on Learning: 
        ◦ Reflect on what you personally learned about logic and critical thinking through this activity.
        ◦ Discuss how this hands-on experience enhanced your understanding compared to simply reading about these concepts.
        ◦ Consider how the chosen activity highlights the application of logic and critical thinking in everyday situations or decision-making.
    5. Conclusion (Brief): Summarize the key takeaways from the activity and its connection to the study of logic and critical thinking.
    6. Deadline for submission of Group written reports, Demonstration and Presentation on Thursday 23 April 2026


 Written Report Structure:
    • Identification and Explanation of Logical Principles: Clear and accurate identification and explanation of at least three relevant logical and critical thinking principles.
    • Concrete Examples and Analysis: Providing specific examples from the group activity to illustrate the logical principles and insightful analysis of their application.
    • Reflection and Learning: Thoughtful and insightful reflection on personal learning and the value of the activity.
    • Clarity, Organization, and Writing Quality: Well-structured report with clear and concise language, proper grammar, and spelling.
Rationale for this Assignment:
    • Active Learning: The hands-on activity promotes active engagement and deeper understanding of abstract logical concepts.
    • Collaborative Learning: Working in groups encourages discussion and different perspectives on how logic is applied.
    • Real-World Connection: Using familiar games makes the application of logic more tangible and relatable to everyday scenarios.
    • Critical Thinking Skills: The planning, execution, and reflection components of the assignment encourage critical thinking, problem-solving, and analytical skills.
    • Communication Skills: The presentation and report components help students develop their communication skills.
